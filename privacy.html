Privacy Policy

Last updated: 10 June 2026

FINOVANTA operates this website to present its software development and IT services, receive professional enquiries and communicate with potential or existing clients. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use and protect personal information when you visit the website, submit a contact request or otherwise communicate with us.

By using this website, you acknowledge that you have read this Privacy Policy and understand how your information may be collected, used and disclosed as described below.

1. Personal information we collect

When we use the term “personal information”, we mean information that identifies you or can reasonably be linked to you. Depending on how you interact with the website, we may collect or process the following categories of information:

  • Contact details, such as your name, company name, email address, phone number and any information you provide in a request form or email.
  • Project and communication information, including the content of messages, service requests, project descriptions, attachments or details you choose to share with us.
  • Technical information, such as your IP address, browser type, device information, operating system, approximate location, access times and pages viewed.
  • Usage information, including how you navigate the website, which services you view and how you interact with forms or buttons.
  • Business transaction information, where applicable, such as proposal details, invoices, payment confirmations or professional correspondence related to a service agreement.

2. Sources of personal information

We may collect personal information directly from you when you submit a form, send us an email, request a service or provide information during a professional exchange. We may also collect limited technical information automatically through the website, hosting infrastructure, analytics tools or security technologies, including cookies or similar technologies where used.

3. How we use your personal information

FINOVANTA may use personal information for the following purposes:

  • Responding to enquiries and communicating with you about services, proposals, timelines and project requirements.
  • Providing and improving services, including software development, web applications, automation systems, API integrations, dashboards, deployment and technical maintenance.
  • Preparing proposals and agreements, managing business communication, issuing invoices and maintaining client records.
  • Website operation and security, including monitoring performance, preventing abuse, detecting technical issues and protecting the website from malicious activity.
  • Legal and administrative purposes, including compliance with applicable laws, accounting obligations, dispute resolution and enforcement of our terms or agreements.
  • Marketing communication, only where permitted by law or where you have provided consent, for example to send relevant updates about FINOVANTA services.

4. How we disclose personal information

We do not sell your personal information. We may disclose personal information only where necessary for legitimate business, technical or legal purposes, including:

  • to hosting providers, IT infrastructure providers, email providers, analytics providers, payment or accounting providers and other vendors that help us operate the website and provide services;
  • to professional advisers, such as accountants, legal advisers or compliance providers, where required for business administration;
  • to public authorities, courts or regulators where required by applicable law or valid legal process;
  • in connection with a business transaction, restructuring or legal obligation, where permitted by law.

5. Third-party websites and links

This website may contain links to third-party websites, platforms or services. We are not responsible for the privacy practices, security or content of third-party websites. If you follow external links, you should review the privacy policies and terms of those services.

6. Cookies and similar technologies

The website may use cookies or similar technologies to provide basic functionality, improve performance, understand usage and protect the website. You can usually manage cookies through your browser settings. Disabling cookies may affect some website features.

7. Children’s data

This website and FINOVANTA’s services are intended for business and professional users. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under the age of majority in their jurisdiction. If you believe that a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will take appropriate steps to delete it.

8. Security and retention

We use reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect personal information. However, no method of transmission or storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

We retain personal information only for as long as necessary for the purposes described in this Privacy Policy, including to respond to enquiries, provide services, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes and maintain business records.

9. Your rights and choices

Depending on where you live, you may have rights in relation to your personal information, including:

  • the right to access or know what personal information we hold about you;
  • the right to request correction of inaccurate personal information;
  • the right to request deletion of personal information;
  • the right to request portability of your personal information;
  • the right to object to or restrict certain processing activities;
  • the right to withdraw consent where processing is based on consent.

These rights may be subject to exceptions and limitations under applicable law. We may need to verify your identity before responding to a request.

10. International transfers

Personal information may be transferred, stored or processed outside the country where you live, including where our service providers operate infrastructure. If personal information is transferred outside the European Economic Area or the United Kingdom, we will rely on recognised transfer mechanisms such as the European Commission’s Standard Contractual Clauses, UK equivalent safeguards or an adequacy decision where applicable.

11. Complaints

If you have concerns about how we process your personal information, you may contact us using the details below. If you are located in the European Economic Area or the United Kingdom, you may also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

12. Changes to this Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, website functionality or legal requirements. The updated version will be posted on this website with a revised “Last updated” date.
